AI policy

Overview

This policy outlines the guidelines for using artificial intelligence (AI) in our school, Gems Our Own Indian School. Our school recognises technology's benefits; to maintain academic integrity, we must comply with plagiarism and exam malpractice guidelines. Additionally, pupil well-being and safe internet use are at the forefront of our considerations. We are also committed to developing our pupils' IT skills and curiosity about the world.

Use of AI for Academic Purposes

AI can be used as an aid for academic purposes, such as research, homework, and assignments. However, it is essential to note that students should not solely rely on AI to complete their work. AI must align with academic integrity guidelines, and students must cite the sources used. Teachers and staff should monitor the use of AI and provide guidance where necessary.

AI chatbots should only be used as supplementary educational tools for enhancing learning and understanding, not as a replacement for independent thought or original work. Students should refer to the course syllabus for specific guidelines on using generative tools in a particular course. Students should seek guidance from their teachers to ensure ethical and responsible use of AI tools in their academic work. Students must acknowledge and provide proper citations for any AI-generated content used in their assignments or projects.

Plagiarism and Copyright Infringement

The use of AI must not result in plagiarism or copyright infringement. Students must understand the concept of plagiarism and be able to use AI ethically. Teachers and staff must educate students on using AI without violating plagiarism guidelines and copyright laws.

Students are expected to use AI ethically. This means that AI should not be harmful, discriminatory, or misleading. Students should not use AI to spread false information or engage in plagiarism, cheating, or unauthorized assistance on assessments or assignments.

Prohibition of AI During Exams

AI must not be used during exams, as this constitutes exam malpractice. Students must refrain from using AI to answer exam questions or seek assistance during the exam. Teachers and staff should ensure that students are aware of this policy and the consequences of violating it.

Our school recognises the benefits of using AI in education and is committed to providing our students with a safe and supportive learning environment. The guidelines outlined in this policy are in place to ensure academic integrity, safeguard students, and maintain ethical standards. Violations of this policy may result in academic penalties or disciplinary actions. By promoting responsible AI use, we aim to equip our students with the necessary skills and values to navigate the evolving technological landscape ethically and responsibly.
